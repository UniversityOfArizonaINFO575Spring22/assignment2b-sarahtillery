Honor Amongst Thieves

An Interactive Multimedia Project
Honor Amongst Thieves would be an all-ages comic story taking inspiration from other multimedia comic works that have seen popular success such as 17776 Football and Homestuck, each which incorporate elements of Google Maps, animation, point and click adventure games, chat logs, and more. The main structure of the story would be a comic, which could be scrolled through, clicked to interact with, and while scrolling and clicking, could activate timed animation sequences, sound effects, planned "deterioration" of the page or content (as an effect of the story), and perhaps more interactive elements not currently planned for. The hope would be to learn how to do this. I've got the story and the art down, next comes the coding!


The Story

Lucie the Gargoyle lives a boring, unstable life as a petty thief, until one day she tries to steal from a man who proves to make thievery an artform--she tricks him into letting her be his apprentice, and the two travel far and wide learning the subtle arts of finery and conning, only to encounter along their travels truths that shake their understanding of the world to its core. A take on Pygmalion, first and foremost, that takes a sharp left turn to somewhere in between Howl's Moving Castle and the hit Ryan Renolds comedy, Free Guy.



Previous Attempts

In my undergraduate degree I attempted with very rudimentary coding, to make an interactive comic for my Advanced Fiction Writing class. It no longer exists, as my coding was fairly basic html supported by a tumblr I no longer have I don't believe, and it was quite a mess. But from that first messy attempt I learned that (which in hindsight should have been obvious), interactive comics are much harder than they seem. Beyond just coding (which from this class I think we all know is very difficult, but rewarding!), you have to consider timing, where your materials are being hosted, what should happen if the materials are viewed on different devices, what should happen if some things break, but others don't, etc. All things I did not consider in my initial attempt.

Although it is still a long way off, this concept of an interactive comic is still one of my top passions and long term goals. I have found that including multimedia narrative forms in Library Science as well has been a treat to explore, and can be incredibly beneficial to improving reading skills and overall comprehension, something I willl explore more in the next page. While I don't imagine my own work could do that, I do think the medium of multimedia and interactive narrative works is something I am very enthusiastic about overall, and would love to contribute to it one day.
